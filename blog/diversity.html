Women in Tech

10.4.15

There have been genuine, public pushes in recent years to encourage diversity in STEM subjects (Science, Technology, Engineering, Mathematics). However, it's clear that as a whole, the mindset of our current working generations can indeed be expanded. I will let this breakdown of employees in the tech industry in 2014 speak for itself.

There is an excellent piece of commentary by Jane Lansing that sums up the silent pains experienced by female tech employees in particular. To quote Lansing,

"It still pains me to remember the time my project manager insisted that a male colleague review the code I’d written for a new project – even though the colleague happened to be notoriously incompetent. Or the times customers couldn’t look me in the eye or even speak to me directly, but would instead ask their questions of my male colleagues – even when it was entirely obvious that the one with the relevant expertise was me."
A recent article by the LA Times highlights the less often talked about issue of qualified, experienced women leaving the tech industry after enduring the undercurrents of years of patriarchal patronizing in the workplace.

Right along the same vein, I came across a moving blog post by a female software engineer who describes her career experiences as "Death by 1,000 paper cuts."

To be frank, this post didn't have to zero in on the tech industry. There is a very real need for kindness and progressive thinking in the world, and I'm truly disappointed to realize how much improving still needs to be done today.
